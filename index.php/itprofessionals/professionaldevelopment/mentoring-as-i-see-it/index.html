  10. Brent Ozar says:

    Prakash – you raise an interesting question: how do you approach a mentor? If you approach a complete stranger and say, “I’d like you to mentor me,” you probably won’t get a positive response.

    As somebody who does a lot of mentoring, I can say that it’s definitely a two-way street. Before I begin a mentoring relationship, I want to see that the person has already taken initiative to contribute in the community. That might be blogging, helping out in forums, speaking at events, answering #SQLHelp questions, or even just being part of a dialog on Twitter. If someone hasn’t done any of those things, I don’t see them as really serious about giving back – only taking. In that case, I personally say no.

    I’d suggest opening your requests by talking about the work you’ve done so far for the community, and then ask for help taking things to the next level. By laying out the work you’ve already done, you can remind your potential mentor that yes, you ARE active even if they don’t remember your name offhand.

    Hope that helps!
