  6. Karen Lopez says:
    April 24, 2012 at 1:52 pm

    The biggest problems most SQLSaturdays have with speaker evals:

    1) Most are use for a draw, so attendees have to put their name and contact information on the form. Most attendees assume, right or wrong, that these go to the organizers. Most have not opted-in to having this information handed over to the speakers.

    2) Since attendees assume the ratings go to the organizers, they often leave comments that the never expect the speaker to see. And yet the speaker sees them and knows who said it. I’ve seen many a speaker room where speakers are reading out some of the worst comments. This is not good.

    3) As a speaker, I have no idea what’s going to happen to the evals, yet I’m usually the one passing them out and collecting them. So I warn people that I might see their contents and that I might be given their contact information. Even if I ask before hand, the end result is often different.

    4) Both the organizers and the speakers should see the comments and ratings. The organizers should be using this as feedback for speaker selection. Speakers want to see how they can improve. That does make more work for the organizers, though.
